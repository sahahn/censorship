nondiscrimination in public schools.

Section A. Chapter 170, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto one new section, to be
known as section 170.346, to read as follows:
170.346. 1. As used in this section, the following terms mean:
(1) "Promote", compelling students, teachers, administrators, or other school
employees to affirm or profess belief in the concepts described in subsection 2 of this
section;
(2) "Public school", the same definition as in section 160.011.
2. No public school shall promote that:
(1) One race or sex is inherently superior to another race or sex;
(2) An individual, solely by virtue of such individual's race or sex, is inherently
racist, sexist, or oppressive;
(3) An individual should be discriminated against or receive adverse treatment
solely or partly because of such individual's race or sex;
(4) An individual's moral character is necessarily determined by such
individual's race or sex;
(5) An individual, solely by virtue of such individual's race or sex, bears
responsibility for actions committed in the past by other members of the same race or
sex;

(6) Any individual, solely by virtue of such individual's race or sex, should feel
discomfort, guilt, anguish, or any other form of psychological distress;
(7) A meritocracy is inherently racist or sexist;
(8) The United States was created by members of a particular race or sex for the
purpose of oppressing members of another race or sex;
(9) The United States government should be violently overthrown;
(10) Particular character traits, values, moral or ethical codes, privileges, or
beliefs should be ascribed to a race or sex or to an individual because of the individual's
race or sex;
(11) The rule of law does not exist, but instead is a series of power relationships
and struggles among racial or other groups;
(12) All Americans are not created equal and are not endowed by their Creator
with certain unalienable rights including life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness; or
(13) Governments should deny to any person within the government's
jurisdiction the equal protection of the law.
3. Public schools shall notify the department of elementary and secondary
education and make general information available on the public school's website, with
detailed information made available upon request, at least thirty days prior to any of the
following:
(1) Providing instruction regarding concepts described in subsection 2 of this
section in curricula, reading lists, seminars, workshops, trainings, or other educational
or professional settings;
(2) Contracting with, hiring, or otherwise engaging speakers, consultants,
diversity trainers, and other persons for the purpose of discussing concepts described in
subsection 2 of this section; or
(3) Contracting with, hiring, or otherwise engaging speakers, consultants,
diversity trainers, and other persons who have previously advocated for the concepts
described in subsection 2 of this section.
4. Subsections 2 and 3 of this section shall not be construed to apply to the
following:
(1) Speech protected by the First Amendment of the Constitution of the United
States;
(2) Using materials, as part of a course of instruction, curriculum, instructional
program, or supplemental instruction, that include the following:
(a) The history of an ethnic group, as described in textbooks and instructional
materials used in public schools;
(b) The impartial discussion of controversial aspects of history;
