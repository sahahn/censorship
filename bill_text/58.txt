AN ACT
To amend chapter 160, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to academic
performance standards and learning standards.
Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the state of Missouri, as follows:
Section A. Chapter 160, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto one new section, to be
2 known as section 160.515, to read as follows:
160.515. 1. In establishing, evaluating, modifying, and revising the academic
2 performance standards and learning standards authorized under sections 160.514 and
3 160.526 as such standards relate to social studies curriculum, the state board of
4 education shall adopt essential knowledge, skills, and competencies necessary to develop
5 each student's civic knowledge including, but not limited to, an understanding of:
6 (1) The fundamental moral, political, and intellectual foundations of the
7 American experiment in self-government;
8 (2) The history of the debate in the First Continental Congress during the
9 formation of the Constitution of the United States;
10 (3) The founding documents of the United States including, but not limited to:
11 (a) The Declaration of Independence;
12 (b) The Constitution of the United States; and
13 (c) The Federalist Papers;
14 (4) The differences between a constitutional republic and a democracy and that
15 the government of the United States is based on a constitutional republican form;
16 (5) The transcript of the first Lincoln-Douglas debate;

41 2. For any social studies course in the required curriculum established under
42 subsection 1 of this section:
43 (1) A teacher shall not be compelled to discuss a particular current event or
44 widely debated and currently controversial issue of public policy or social affairs;
45 (2) A teacher who chooses to discuss a topic described under subdivision (1) of
46 this subsection shall, to the best of the teacher's ability, strive to explore the topic from
47 diverse and contending perspectives without giving deference to any one perspective;
48 (3) A school district, open-enrollment charter school, or teacher shall not
49 require, make part of a course, award a grade or course credit, or award extra credit for
50 a student's:
51 (a) Political activism, lobbying, or efforts to persuade members of the legislative
52 or executive branch at the federal, state, or local level to take specific actions by direct
53 communication; or

54 (b) Participation in any internship, practicum, or similar activity involving
55 social or public policy advocacy; and
56 (4) A teacher, administrator, or other employee of a state agency, school district,
57 or open-enrollment charter school shall not:
58 (a) Be required to engage in training, orientation, or therapy that presents any
59 form of race or sex stereotyping or blame on the basis of race or sex; or
60 (b) Require or make part of a course the concept that:
61 a. One race or sex is inherently superior to another race or sex;
62 b. An individual, by virtue of the individual's race or sex, is inherently racist,
63 sexist, or oppressive, whether consciously or unconsciously;
64 c. An individual should be discriminated against or receive adverse treatment
65 solely or partly because of the individual's race;
66 d. An individual's moral character, standing, or worth is necessarily determined
67 by the individual's race or sex;
68 e. An individual, by virtue of the individual's race or sex, bears responsibility for
69 actions committed in the past by other members of the same race or sex;
70 f. An individual should feel discomfort, guilt, anguish, or any other form of
71 psychological distress on account of the individual's race or sex;
72 g. Meritocracy or traits such as a strong work ethic are racist or sexist or were
73 created by members of a particular race to oppress members of another race;
74 h. The advent of slavery in the territory that is now the United States constituted
75 the true founding of the United States; or
76 i. With respect to their relationship to American values, slavery and racism are
77 anything other than deviations from, betrayals of, or failures to live up to the authentic
78 founding principles of the United States, which include liberty and equality.
79 3. A state agency, school district, or open-enrollment charter school shall not
80 accept private funding for the purpose of developing a curriculum, purchasing or
81 selecting curriculum materials, or providing teacher training or professional
82 development for a course described in subdivision (3) of subsection 2 of this section.
83 4. A school district or open-enrollment charter school shall not implement,
84 interpret, or enforce any rules or student code of conduct in a manner that results in the
85 punishment of a student for discussing, or has a chilling effect on student discussion of,
86 the concepts described in subdivision (4) of subsection 2 of this section.
