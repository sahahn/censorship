Section 1. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS.
1. The general assembly finds that recently developed
United States history curriculum derived from a project by the
New York Times, known as the “1619 Project”, attempts to deny
or obfuscate the fundamental principles upon which the United
States was founded.
2. The general assembly has a strong interest in promoting
an accurate account of this nation’s history through public
schools and forming young people into knowledgeable and
patriotic citizens.
Sec. 2. Section 256.11, Code 2021, is amended by adding the
following new subsection:
NEW SUBSECTION. 5A. A school shall not utilize any United
States history curriculum that in whole or in part is derived
from a project by the New York Times, known as the “1619
Project”, or any similarly developed curriculum.
Sec. 3. Section 257.7, Code 2021, is amended by adding the
following new subsection:
NEW SUBSECTION. 3. Budget reductions for curriculum
violations. The amount of a school district’s budget
otherwise determined under subsection 1 shall be reduced by
one one-hundred-eightieth for each day of the previous budget
year for which the school district used school curriculum in
violation of section 256.11, subsection 5A.
Sec. 4. NEW SECTION. 257.17A Aid reduction for curriculum
violations.
1. State aid payments made pursuant to section 257.16 for a
fiscal year shall be reduced by one one-hundred-eightieth for
each day of that fiscal year for which the school district used
school curriculum in violation of section 256.11, subsection
5A, during the prior school year.
2. The department of management shall prorate the reduction
made pursuant to this section to account for a school district
that is approved to maintain a year-round school calendar under
section 279.10, subsection 2.

Sec. 5. Section 260C.17, subsection 1, Code 2021, is amended
to read as follows:

an annual budget designating the proposed expenditures for

This bill includes findings of the general assembly that
the recently developed United States history curriculum
derived from a project by the New York Times, known as the
“1619 Project”, attempts to deny or obfuscate the fundamental
principles upon which the United States was founded and that
the general assembly has a strong interest in promoting an
accurate account of this nation’s history through public
schools and forming young people into knowledgeable and
patriotic citizens.
The bill amends provisions governing the educational program
established by the state board of education to prohibit a
school from utilizing any United States history curriculum that
in whole or in part is derived from a project by the New York
Times, known as the “1619 Project”, or any similarly developed
curriculum. The bill requires reductions to the authorized
budget of and state aid payments to a school district that
violates the prohibition on the use of prohibited curriculum.

The bill also provides that if a merged area (community
college) has utilized any United States history curriculum
during the previous fiscal year that in whole or in part is
derived from a project by the New York Times, known as the
“1619 Project”, or any similarly developed curriculum, the
state board shall reduce the merged area’s final total budget
by an amount proportionate to the number of days during that
fiscal year that the curriculum was used.
Under the bill, if an institution under the control of
the board of regents has utilized any United States history
curriculum during the previous fiscal year that in whole or in
part is derived from a project by the New York Times, known as
the “1619 Project”, or any similarly developed curriculum, the
monthly appropriations paid to the institution shall be reduced
by an amount proportionate to the number of days during that
fiscal year that the curriculum was used.
The bill takes effect upon enactment and applies to budgets,
state aid payments, and appropriations for fiscal years
beginning on or after July 1, 2021.
