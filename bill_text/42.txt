
MISSISSIPPI LEGISLATURE

2022 Regular Session

To: Education

By: Senator(s) Fillingane

Senate Bill 2106

AN ACT TO AMEND SECTION 37-13-3, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972,
TO PROVIDE THAT ALL COUNTY SUPERINTENDENTS OF EDUCATION AND SUPERINTENDENTS OF MUNICIPAL
SEPARATE SCHOOL DISTRICTS SHALL NOT ALLOW THE TEACHING OR INSTRUCTION OF STUDENTS
IN CERTAIN DIVISIVE CONCEPTS REGARDING RACE, GENDER, SEX, SOCIAL CLASS OR POLITICAL
AFFILIATION; TO REQUIRE THE STATE DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION REDUCE FUNDING TO ELEMENTARY
AND SECONDARY SCHOOLS THAT TEACH DIVISIVE CONCEPTS; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES.

BE IT ENACTED BY THE
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI:

SECTION 1.  Section  37-13-3, Mississippi Code of 1972, is amended as follows:

37-13-3.  No  doctrinal, sectarian or denominational teaching shall be permitted in public  schools of this state.  It shall be the duty of the county superintendents of  education and the superintendents of municipal separate school districts to  enforce the provisions of this section.

(1)

(2)  All county
superintendents of education and superintendents of municipal separate school
districts shall not allow the instruction, training or teaching of students of the
following divisive concepts:

(a)  That one race or
sex is inherently superior to another race or sex;

(b)  That this state
or the United States is fundamentally racist or sexist;

(c)  That an individual,
by virtue of his or her race or sex, is inherently racist, sexist or oppressive,
whether consciously or unconsciously;

(d)  That an individual
should be discriminated against or receive adverse treatment solely or partly because
of his or her race or sex;

(e)  That an individual's
moral character is determined by his or her race or sex;

(f)  That an individual,
by virtue of his or her race or sex, bears responsibility for actions committed
in the past by other members of the same race or sex; and

(g)  That an individual
should feel discomfort, guilt, anguish or any other form of psychological distress
on account of his or her race or sex.

(3)  All county
superintendents of education and superintendents of municipal separate school
districts shall not allow a course, class, event or activity within its schools'
program of instruction that:

(a)  Promotes the overthrow
of the United States Government;

(b)  Promotes division
between, resentment of, or social justice against a:

(i)  Race;

(ii)  Gender;

(iii)  Political
affiliation;

(iv)  Social class;
or

(v)  A particular
class of people;

(c)  Negatively targets
specific nationalities or countries.

(4)  (a)  If an elementary
school or secondary school that receives state funding violates subsections (2)
or (3) of this section, the State Department of Education shall determine the costs
associated with the teaching or instruction of these concepts, including planning
time and teaching time.

(b)  The State Department
of Education shall reduce funds provided to an elementary or secondary school in
an amount equal to the cost amount determined under Subsection (4)(a) of this Section.

(5)  If an elementary school
or secondary school violates subsections (2) or (3) of this section, the State Department
of Education shall reduce the school's allotment of funds from the Mississippi Adequate
Education Program by twenty-five percent (25%) for each year that the school violates
subsections (3) or (4) of this section.

SECTION 2.  This act  shall take effect and be in force from and after July 1, 2022.
