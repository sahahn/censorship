Section A. Chapter 170, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto one new section, to be
known as section 170.347, to read as follows:
170.347. 1. As used in this section, the following terms mean:
(1) "Promote", compelling students to affirm or profess belief in the concepts or
ideas described in subsection 2 of this section;
(2) "Public school", the same definition as in section 160.011;
(3) "State exam", any examination required as provided in section 170.011,
170.013, or 170.345 or that is produced by the department of elementary and secondary
education for assessment purposes as provided in section 160.526.
2. No state exam shall include any question or statement that promotes any
concept or idea that:
(1) One race or sex is inherently superior to another race or sex;
(2) An individual, solely by virtue of such individual's race or sex, is inherently
racist, sexist, or oppressive, whether consciously or unconsciously;
(3) An individual should be discriminated against or receive adverse treatment
solely or partly because of such individual's race or sex;
(4) An individual's moral character, standing, or worth is necessarily determined
by such individual's race or sex;

(5) An individual, solely by virtue of such individual's race or sex, bears
responsibility for actions committed in the past by other members of the same race or
sex;
(6) An individual, solely by virtue of such individual's race or sex, should feel
discomfort, guilt, anguish, or any other form of psychological distress;
(7) A meritocracy or traits such as a strong work ethic are inherently racist or
sexist or were created by members of a particular race to oppress members of another
race;
(8) The advent of slavery in the territory that is now the United States
constituted the true founding of the United States; or
(9) Slavery and racism, with respect to their relationship to American values, are
anything other than deviations from, betrayals of, or failures to live up to the authentic
founding principles of the United States, which include liberty and equality.
3. If any nationwide standardized test includes any question promoting the
concepts or ideas described in subsection 2 of this section, no student in this state shall
be required to take such test but may substitute any other equivalent nationwide
standardized test that does not promote such concepts or ideas.
